Office Expressions

Below are 20 full sentences using expressions common in British office and legal settings.

  1. I'm just popping off to make some tea; would you like anything from the kitchen?
  2. Could you please phone later, or leave your name and email address, and we will make sure to contact you as soon as the lawyer is back in the office?
  3. I'll send you an email with the documents after lunch.
  4. I'll give you a bell this afternoon to go over the details.
  5. I'm tied up in meetings all morning, but I can see you after one o'clock.
  6. I'll nip out for a sandwich and be back by two.
  7. Please put the file on my desk when you have a moment.
  8. We need to carry out a due diligence before advising the client on the acquisition.
  9. The correspondence was marked without prejudice to allow frank settlement talks.
  10. The firm agreed to take the matter on pro bono for the local charity.
  11. Please ensure client confidentiality is maintained at all times when handling those papers.
  12. Could you draft a letter of intent to record the parties' preliminary agreement?
  13. I'll pop the contract in the post this afternoon so it reaches the court tomorrow.
  14. Let's have a quick word in the corridor about the timetable.
  15. I'll be out of the office on Friday but available by email if the matter is urgent.
  16. Can you chase that up the supplier and let me know the outcome?
  17. We'll put the proposal on the agenda for the next board meeting.
  18. By close of business, please submit your weekly timesheets.
  19. There are three action points from today's meeting that the team must complete.
  20. I'll follow up with the client first thing tomorrow to confirm the arrangements.
